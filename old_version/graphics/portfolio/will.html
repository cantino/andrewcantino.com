Final Porfolio
Will Moss

This is Will's final portfolio for Computer Graphics at Swarthmore College.


A bluescreened image of Will over a photograph.
We removed the blue background from the original image, and then superimposed the resulting image over the photograph.


A simple optical illusion.
This was done using only our line drawing routine and a series of for loops.


A table and a poster board.
I used our polygon and circle scanline fill algonithm to fill all the shapes in the iamge. All the textures were mapped on using simple pattern mapping.


Faded Circles.
These circles were also drawn using our scanline fill algorithm. This time I made use of our alpha mask, and our circular alpha gradients. I then drew circles randomly on the screen.


X-Wings on Patrol.
This image used the heirarchical modeling system to generate a complex animated scene. The x-wing was generated out of two wing models, a body, and four guns. Then a fleet could easily be constructed out of five x-wings, and animated.


A torus.
We implemented a system for making polygon meshes out of paramaterized surfaces. I gave it a torus in this case.


A superellipsoid.
This time I passed I function for a superellisoid, and animated it by varying the e1 and e2 parameters.


A model of an eagle.
This model contains over 30,000 polygons, and is rendered using our engine. The model was imported using code we wrote to import ase format polygon meshes. The animation was made by placing the model into our heirarchical modeling system and transforming it as we would anything else.


Spacetron!
This is another image modeling using our model import code. This image contained over 50,000 polygons from 5 separate models.


Sheet hanging out
This image was generated using our physics engine and a sheet of points. The points were connected by polygons for rendering. The top row of points was fixed, and after the sheet has settled under world gravity, the bottom row of points is suddenly displaced down and back.


Hanging a torus
In this image we using our parameterized function object to generate a torus, and then imported it into our physics engine. Every point is connected to every other point in the torus by a spring, whose spring constant varies inversly with the distance. Then one point on the torus is fixed, and it is allowed to fall under world gravity.
